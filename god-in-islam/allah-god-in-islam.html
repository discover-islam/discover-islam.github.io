Allah (God) in Islam

Islam is the complete submission and obedience to Allah (God). The name Allah in Islam never refers to Muhammad — Allah is the personal name of God.

Islam is the complete submission and obedience to Allah (God). The name Allah in Islam never refers to Muhammad (peace be upon him), as many Christians may mistakenly think. Allah is the personal name of God, used by Muslims of all languages, including Arab Christians.

What do Muslims believe about Allah? He is the one God, Who has no partner. Nothing is like Him. He is the Creator, not created, nor a part of His creation. He is All-Powerful, absolutely Just. There is no other entity in the entire universe worthy of worship besides Him.

He is First, Last, and Everlasting. He was when nothing else was, and will be when nothing else remains. He is the All-Knowing, All-Merciful, the Supreme, the Sovereign. It is only He Who is capable of granting life to anything.

He sent His Messengers (peace be upon them) to guide all of mankind. He sent Muhammad (peace be upon him) as the last Prophet and Messenger for all mankind. His book is the Holy Quran, the only authentic revealed book in the world preserved without change.

Muslims follow several guidelines in their understanding of God: They eliminate any anthropomorphism — God's attributes are not like human attributes. They have unwavering faith in exactly what Allah and Prophet Muhammad described Allah to be — no more, no less. They accept all of God's names and attributes without analogy or comparison.

Similarity in names does not imply similarity in what is described. As a robotic arm differs from a human arm, the 'hand' of Allah is not like the hand of a human being. Allah tells us about Himself in the Quran: 'There is nothing whatever like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing.' (42:11)
