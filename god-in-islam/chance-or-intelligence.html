Chance or Intelligence? The Evidence for God

This universe displays remarkable order and consistency. Could such encompassing order have arisen by accident, or does it point to an intelligent Creator?

This Universe indeed displays a remarkable vista of order and consistency. We can observe the great regularity with which celestial bodies follow precise orbits, and marvel at how water from the seas is brought to the land by wind-driven clouds, replenishing life across the earth.

We are amazed by the migration patterns of many species — newborn eels that trek through thousands of miles of ocean to reach their local streams, and bees that use sunlight for navigation in their quest for nectar. We may ponder why all living things exist in pairs, and contemplate the optimality of the processes that comprise the whole universe.

Even incredibly minute changes would disrupt the balances in nature. Had the earth's orbit been slightly offset in either direction, water and the resulting forms of life would not have emerged. Through the spectrum of life and non-life — from subatomic particles to the macrocosmic expanse of the universe — integration is manifested on every level.

This reflection leads us to pertinent questions: Could such encompassing order have arisen purely by unintentional accident? Or could there be an intelligent Originator to this spectacular array of living and non-living forms — a designer who developed them through processes that may yet be fully understood?

Is it probable that all the letters on a page unscrambled themselves by chance to form meaningful and structured sentences? How then could a human being — with ears, eyes, and a mind — have been formed by chance? Is it credible that a vast universe with inestimable billions of galaxies could have evolved by accident?

Does not the integration and complexity of nature point to a Maker of unparalleled intelligence, power, and design? The Quran poses this question: 'Were they created out of nothing, or were they themselves the creators?' (52:35). The universe, in its breathtaking order, is itself a testament to the existence of the One Who created it.
