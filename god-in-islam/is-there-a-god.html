Is There a God?

A logical and accessible argument for the existence of God, using evidence from design, creation, and the consistent order of the universe.

Consider this: An archaeologist digs deep into the desert sand and finds a piece of an old clay pot. From this little piece of dusty clay, this archaeologist can tell us about the civilization that existed thousands of years ago — the types of ovens, temperatures, and dyes they worked with, the raw materials they used, and the level of their artistic skill. All this from a small piece of clay.

Did this archaeologist ever see the civilization that produced this pot? No. How does he know it ever existed? He knows because he sees that the piece of clay was produced by someone who designed it, shaped it, and had the intelligence to heat and color it. The existence of the pot is conclusive proof of the existence of the people who made it.

Look around you. The sun, moon, stars — billions of galaxies arranged in breathtaking precision. Plants and animals — each species perfectly adapted to its environment. The human body — with 37 trillion cells, each more complex than any city, working in perfect coordination. Did all this come about by accident?

A design needs a designer. An effect needs a cause. When we see complexity, order, and purpose in the world, we naturally infer an intelligence behind it. This is not just philosophical reasoning — it is the basis of all scientific inquiry.
