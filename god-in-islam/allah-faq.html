Questions About Allah — Frequently Asked

Answers to common philosophical and theological questions about the nature of God in Islam, including questions about creation, omnipotence, and how we know God exists.

Q: 'If God created everything, then who created God?' A: According to the Quran, Allah tells us that He is the only Creator and Sustainer of all that exists. He is not created — He is unlike His creation in every way. He calls Himself Al-Awwal (The First), Al-Akhir (The Last), and As-Samad (The Eternal, sought by all creation while He has need of none). He always has existed and was never created.

Q: 'How can you believe in God when you can't see, hear, or touch Him?' A: We know from Islamic teaching that no one has seen God in this lifetime. However, we are encouraged to use our senses and reason to recognize that this universe could not possibly have come into existence on its own. We don't have to see an artist to recognize a painting. Similarly, we can believe Allah created everything without having to see Him.

Q: 'Can God do anything — can He make a rock so big He can't move it?' A: Allah tells us He is capable of doing anything He wills to do. However, Allah does not will to do self-contradictory things. The question contains a logical contradiction — it's not a limitation of God's power but a limitation of the question itself.

Q: 'Why does God allow suffering in the world?' A: Islam teaches that this world is a test. Suffering serves multiple purposes: it purifies the believer, tests patience and gratitude, and provides opportunities for others to help and show compassion. The Quran assures: 'Verily, with hardship comes ease.' (94:5-6). Ultimate justice will be served on the Day of Judgment.

Q: 'Are there other gods besides Allah?' A: In Islam, there is absolutely and unequivocally only One God. The Quran states: 'If there were, in the heavens and the earth, other gods besides Allah, there would have been confusion and corruption in both!' (21:22). The existence of a perfectly ordered universe, governed by consistent laws, points to One Omnipotent Creator.
