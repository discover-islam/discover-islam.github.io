Where is Allah?

The Islamic understanding of where Allah is — based on the Quran, the Sunnah, and the consensus of early Muslim scholars.

The question of where Allah is — concerning His highness above His creation — is one of the most important theological questions in Islam. The correct belief, based on the Quran and the authentic Sunnah, is that Allah is above His creation, above the heavens, above His Throne.

The Quran states: 'The Most Merciful ascended above the Throne.' (20:5). And: 'To Him ascend the good words, and the righteous deed raises them.' (35:10). The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) himself confirmed this when he asked a slave girl: 'Where is Allah?' and she said: 'Above the heavens.' He declared her a believer.

The companions of the Prophet and the early scholars of Islam were unanimous in affirming that Allah is above His creation, above His Throne, in a manner that befits His Majesty. This does not mean He is confined within the physical heavens — rather, His 'aboveness' is absolute and beyond human comprehension.

At the same time, Allah is All-Knowing of everything happening everywhere, and His knowledge, power, and awareness encompass all of creation. The Quran says: 'And He is with you wherever you are.' (57:4) — meaning His knowledge and awareness are with us, not that He is physically present in every place.

This belief — that Allah is above His creation and that He is All-Knowing of all things — fills the believer with reverence and awe. It gives direction to prayer (Muslims raise their hands and hearts toward Allah above), it inspires humility, and it reassures the believer that Allah sees and knows all, missing nothing.
