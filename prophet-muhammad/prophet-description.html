Description of the Prophet Muhammad

A detailed description of the Prophet Muhammad's physical appearance, dress, manners, family life, and daily conduct.

Muhammad (peace be upon him) was of a height a little above average, with a sturdy build and long muscular limbs. His hair was long and thick with some waves. His forehead was large and prominent, his eyelashes long and thick, his nose sloping, his mouth somewhat large, and his teeth well-set. He had a pleasant smile and large, dark eyes with a touch of brown.

His gait was firm and he walked so fast that others found it difficult to keep pace with him. His face was genial but at times, when deep in thought, there were long periods of silence, yet he always kept himself occupied. He did not speak unnecessarily and what he said was always to the point. His laugh was mostly a smile. He kept his feelings under firm control.

His dress generally consisted of a shirt, trousers, a shawl thrown around his shoulders, and a turban. On rare occasions he would wear more elaborate robes presented by foreign emissaries. His blanket had several patches. He had very few spare clothes, but kept them spotlessly clean. He would remark to others: 'Why can't you wash your clothes?'

His mode of living was simple almost to the point of austerity. He slept on a mat on the floor. His pillow was stuffed with palm tree fibers. He kept no more food stored than was immediately necessary. He would often go without food for days. 'I fast,' he once said, 'as I do not find food.'

His manners were exemplary. He was kind and gentle with everyone. He would visit the sick, attend funerals, accept invitations from the poor, mend his own clothes, milk his own goats, and help with household chores. He never made anyone feel unimportant. Children loved him and he loved them.

His justice was total and impartial. He said: 'If my daughter Fatima had committed theft, I would have cut her hand off.' His compassion was boundless — he wept for the suffering of others, interceded for the weak, and protected the rights of women, the poor, orphans, and non-Muslims under Islamic governance.
