The Prophet's Compassion for Children

The Prophet Muhammad demonstrated extraordinary love and care for children, transforming the norms of his society to protect and honor the youngest members of the community.

In pre-Islamic Arabia, the status of children — especially girls — was precarious. Female infanticide was practiced, children had few rights, and orphans were routinely exploited. The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) transformed this situation completely, establishing rights for children that were revolutionary for his time.

The Prophet's love for children was evident in every aspect of his life. He would interrupt his prayers when he heard a baby crying, to reassure the mother. He would carry children on his shoulders during walks. He would race with them and let them win. He said: 'He is not of us who does not have mercy on our young ones.'

He established comprehensive rights for children: the right to be acknowledged and named, the right to be nursed and cared for, the right to education, the right to fair treatment among siblings, and the right to inherit. He said: 'Treat your children equally in gifts — for if I were to give preference to any, I would prefer women.'

The Prophet condemned female infanticide in the strongest terms, and the Quran directly addresses this: 'And when the girl (who was buried alive) is asked: For what sin was she killed?' (81:8-9). This divine indictment transformed Arab society's attitude toward daughters.

The Prophet himself experienced the joy and pain of parenthood deeply. He had seven children and wept openly at their deaths. When his son Ibrahim died in infancy, he said: 'Our eyes shed tears and our hearts grieve, but we do not say anything against the will of our Lord.'

The Prophet's model of engaged, loving, fair, and respectful fatherhood remains a powerful counter-example to both harsh authoritarianism and permissive neglect. He showed that true love for children means caring for their moral and spiritual development alongside their physical needs.
