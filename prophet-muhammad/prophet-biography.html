The Prophet of Islam: His Biography

A comprehensive overview of the life of Prophet Muhammad — from his birth in Mecca to his migration to Medina and the final years of his life.

Muhammad ibn Abdullah was born in Mecca, Arabia, around 570 CE, in the year of the Elephant — named for an Abyssinian army that attempted to destroy the Kaaba using war elephants but was miraculously repelled. His father Abdullah died before his birth; his mother Amina died when he was six. He was raised first by his grandfather Abd al-Muttalib, and after his death, by his uncle Abu Talib.

Muhammad grew up to become known throughout Mecca as 'Al-Amin' (The Trustworthy) and 'Al-Sadiq' (The Truthful). He worked as a trader and at the age of 25 married Khadijah bint Khuwaylid, a wealthy widow 15 years his senior who had employed him. Their marriage was deeply loving and Khadijah remained his only wife for 25 years until her death.

At the age of 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira during the month of Ramadan, Muhammad received his first revelation: 'Read in the name of your Lord who created. Created man from a clot. Read, and your Lord is the Most Generous.' (96:1-3). This began his 23-year mission as the final prophet of God.

The early years of his mission in Mecca were marked by brutal persecution. His followers were tortured, killed, and forced to migrate. In 622 CE, God commanded the migration (Hijra) to Medina — this event marks the beginning of the Islamic calendar. In Medina, Muhammad established the world's first pluralistic constitutional state, with the Charter of Medina guaranteeing rights to all citizens regardless of religion.

In 630 CE, he returned to Mecca with 10,000 followers in a peaceful conquest, forgiving all his enemies. In his final years, he systematized the institutions of Islamic governance, worship, family, and society. He passed away in 632 CE in Medina, having transformed Arabia — and set in motion a transformation of the world.
