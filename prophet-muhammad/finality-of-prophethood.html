The Finality of Prophethood

The Islamic belief that Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the final prophet and messenger of God, completing and sealing all previous divine messages.

One of the foundational beliefs of Islam is that Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the last and final prophet. The Quran states clearly: 'Muhammad is not the father of any of your men, but he is the Messenger of Allah and the Last of the Prophets.' (33:40)

This concept of the 'Seal of the Prophets' (Khatam al-Nabiyeen) means that no new prophet or messenger will come after Muhammad. The divine message — delivered through thousands of prophets across human history — has been completed and perfected in the Quran and the Sunnah of Muhammad.

The finality of prophethood is not a limitation but a completion. The Quran, as the final revelation, is preserved in its original form and is comprehensive in its guidance. Unlike previous scriptures that have been altered over time, the Quran remains unchanged and applicable to all times and places.

This belief means that any claim to prophethood after Muhammad is to be rejected. The Quran is the final scripture and Muhammad's example (Sunnah) provides the practical model for living according to divine guidance. Together, they constitute a complete and perfect framework for human life.

The coming of Muhammad fulfilled prophecies found in previous scriptures. The Bible, in its original form, contained references to the coming of a final prophet. Several passages in both the Old and New Testaments are understood by Muslim scholars to refer to Muhammad — including Jesus's promise of the 'Paraclete' or 'Comforter' who would come after him.
