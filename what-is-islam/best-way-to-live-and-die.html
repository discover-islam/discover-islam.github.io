The Best Way to Live and Die

A personal account of a man's journey from a hedonistic American lifestyle to finding peace and purpose in Islam.

Every day the sun shines on new people recognizing the truth and returning to their Creator in repentance and submission to Him. They are ambitious for God's reward of eternal happiness in Paradise. These new followers bring a new flavor to the Muslims of today with their sincerity, keenness, and solid adherence to the teaching of Islam.

I once thought my upbringing offered an excellent way of life, especially since I felt satisfied both mentally and physically. As a young man, I lived the life of an average American who had a rather hedonistic lifestyle. I was fond of music, a festive atmosphere, sports, travel, ethnic foods, and foreign languages. I reached a point, however, where I felt 'spiritually bankrupt' and I asked myself: 'Now what?' And I thought: 'There has to be more to life than this.'

This realization was the catalyst that led me to search for the truth through diverse avenues. I began reading, attending church services, meditating, and exploring various philosophical systems. Each path offered something, but none offered the complete, coherent framework for life that I was seeking.

When I first encountered Islam, I was struck by the clarity and comprehensiveness of its worldview. Here was a religion that addressed not just spiritual concerns but every aspect of human existence — economics, law, family, ethics, politics — with a consistency and wisdom that I found profoundly compelling.

The concept of tawheed — the absolute Oneness of God — resonated deeply with me. It was a clear, rational, and spiritually satisfying concept that eliminated the confusion I had felt in other theological frameworks. The idea that God is One, that He needs nothing from us, and that our worship is for our own benefit — this made profound sense.

After careful study and reflection, I took the Shahada — the declaration of faith. In that moment, a profound peace descended on me that I had never experienced before. I understood then why the Arabic word for Islam also means 'peace'. True peace comes from submitting one's will to the will of the Creator. — Donald W. Flood (Yahya)
