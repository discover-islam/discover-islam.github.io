What is Islam?

Islam is not a new religion, but the same truth that God revealed through all His prophets to every people. For a fifth of the world's population, Islam is both a religion and a complete way of life.

Islam is not a new religion, but the same truth that God revealed through all His prophets to every people. For a fifth of the world's population, Islam is both a religion and a complete way of life. Muslims follow a religion of peace, mercy, and forgiveness, and the majority have nothing to do with the extremely grave events which have come to be associated with their faith.

The word 'Islam' itself means 'submission to God' and 'peace'. A Muslim is one who submits to God and lives in peace. Islam teaches that God — called Allah in Arabic — is One, Eternal, and Unique. He has no partners, no equals, and no offspring. He is the Creator and Sustainer of all that exists.

Islam emphasizes that all human beings are equal before God, regardless of race, nationality, or social status. The only distinction that matters is piety and righteousness. This revolutionary concept of equality was a cornerstone of the Islamic message from its very beginning.

Muslims believe in all the prophets sent by God, including Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus, and Muhammad (peace be upon them all). Muhammad (peace be upon him) is considered the final prophet and messenger, completing and confirming all previous divine messages.

The Islamic way of life encompasses not just religious rituals but also guides social behavior, economics, law, ethics, and family life. It provides a comprehensive framework for human existence, addressing every aspect of life from personal hygiene to international relations.

Islam is the fastest-growing religion in the world today, with over 1.8 billion adherents across every continent. This growth reflects the universal appeal of its message of monotheism, justice, and compassion.
