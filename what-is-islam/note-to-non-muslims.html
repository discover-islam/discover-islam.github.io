A Note to Our Non-Muslim Visitors

A sincere invitation to explore Islam with an open mind, emphasizing that there is no compulsion in religion and encouraging honest intellectual inquiry.

We want to remind you that there is no compulsion for anyone to accept the truth. But it is certainly a shame upon the human intellect when a person is not even interested in finding out what the truth is. Islam teaches that God has given every person the faculty of reason and expects us to use it — to reflect, to question, and to reflect again.

Nobody should press you to make a hasty decision about any of the teachings of Islam. Islam teaches that every person should be given the freedom to choose. Even when faced with the truth, there is no compulsion to embrace it. The Quran explicitly states: 'Let there be no compulsion in religion: Truth stands out clearly from Error.' (2:256)

But before you form an opinion about Islam, ask yourself honestly: is your existing knowledge of Islam thorough enough? Has that knowledge come from reliable primary sources, or from third-party accounts that may themselves be based on incomplete or biased information? Would you form an opinion about the taste of a dish based only on hearsay from someone who has never tasted it?

We invite you to learn about Islam from its primary sources — the Quran and the authentic teachings of the Prophet Muhammad. Read, reflect, and reason. Ask questions. Engage with the material honestly and objectively. Give it the same intellectual rigor you would apply to any important question in your life.

Islam is not merely an Eastern religion or a cultural tradition. It is a universal message addressed to all of humanity. Its core teachings — the Oneness of God, moral accountability, justice, compassion, and human dignity — speak to the deepest aspirations of the human spirit.

May your intellectual journey toward the truth be a pleasant and fruitful one. We believe that sincere, honest inquiry can only lead to greater light.
