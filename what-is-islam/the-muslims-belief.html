The Muslim's Belief

A comprehensive overview of Islamic creed, covering belief in God's Oneness, the Quran and Sunnah, angels, prophets, the Day of Judgment, and divine decree.

The foundation of the Muslim's belief rests on six articles of faith. These are: belief in Allah (God), belief in His angels, belief in His revealed books, belief in His messengers, belief in the Last Day (Day of Judgment), and belief in divine decree — whether good or bad.

Belief in Allah means acknowledging His absolute Oneness (Tawheed), His perfect attributes, and His lordship over all creation. He has no partners, no children, and nothing resembles Him. He is the First without beginning and the Last without end, the Evident and the Hidden.

Muslims believe in angels as honorable beings created from light who worship Allah without ceasing and carry out His commands perfectly. The most well-known angels include Gabriel (who brought revelation), Michael (responsible for provisions), Israfil (who will blow the trumpet on the Day of Judgment), and Azrael (the angel of death).

Muslims believe in all divinely revealed books, including the Torah given to Moses, the Psalms to David, the Gospel to Jesus, and the Quran to Muhammad (peace be upon them). However, only the Quran remains in its original, unaltered form as a guide for all of humanity.

The belief in all messengers — from Adam to Muhammad — is essential to the Muslim faith. Prophets were humans, not divine, chosen by Allah to convey His message. They possessed the highest moral character and were protected from major sins. Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the seal of all prophets.

The Day of Judgment is when Allah will resurrect all people and hold them accountable for their deeds. The righteous will enter Paradise and the wrongdoers — unless forgiven — will face punishment. Muslims believe that this life is a test and preparation for eternal life.

Belief in divine decree means that Allah has full knowledge of all things before they occur and has recorded everything in a preserved tablet. Yet humans have free will and are responsible for their own choices. This belief brings both comfort in hardship and gratitude in good times.
