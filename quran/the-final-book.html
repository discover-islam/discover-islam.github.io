The Quran: The Final Book

Some people reject God because they can find no evidence that satisfies both their hearts and minds. The Quran is the key that satisfies both.

Some people reject God because they can find no evidence that satisfies both their hearts and minds simultaneously. Then there are others who accept God on the basis of what they feel in their hearts, without the rigorous intellectual engagement they might apply to other questions. The Quran challenges both groups — it speaks to both the heart and the intellect simultaneously.

The Quran describes itself as 'a guidance for the people and clear proofs of guidance and criterion.' (2:185). It is not merely a book of religious rituals but a comprehensive guide for human life, a source of scientific reflection, a treasury of historical narrative, and an inexhaustible well of spiritual wisdom.

The Quran was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad over 23 years, beginning in 610 CE and concluding with his death in 632 CE. It was memorized by thousands of companions during the Prophet's lifetime and is the most memorized book in the world today, with millions who can recite it completely from memory.

Unlike any other religious scripture, the Quran has been preserved in its exact original form since its revelation. The same Arabic text that the Prophet recited is the text recited by Muslims around the world today. No other book of such antiquity can make this claim.

The Quran presents itself as the final revelation from God to humanity — the completion and confirmation of all previous divine messages. It affirms the Torah, the Psalms, and the Gospel as originally revealed books, while noting that they have been altered from their original forms over time. The Quran, by contrast, was divinely protected from alteration.

For Muslims, the Quran is not merely a historical document but a living reality — the direct word of God, speaking to every generation with fresh insight and guidance. Its recitation is an act of worship; its study, a lifelong journey; its application, the path to peace and success in both worlds.
