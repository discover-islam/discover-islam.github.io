Scientific Miracles in the Quran

The Quran contains numerous statements about the natural world that have been verified by modern science — from cosmology to embryology, oceanography to meteorology.

The Quran was revealed 1,400 years ago to a man who could neither read nor write, in a society without any scientific institutions or advanced knowledge of natural phenomena. And yet the Quran contains statements about the natural world that have only been confirmed by modern science centuries after the Quran's revelation. This is one of the strongest evidences for its divine origin.

Cosmology: The Quran describes the Big Bang: 'Do not those who disbelieve see that the heavens and the earth were a closed-up mass, then We opened them out?' (21:30). It describes the expanding universe: 'We have built the heaven with might, and verily We are expanding it.' (51:47). Both were confirmed only in the 20th century.

Embryology: The Quran describes the stages of human embryonic development with remarkable accuracy: 'Then We placed him as a drop in a place of settlement, firmly fixed. Then We made the drop into an alaqah (clinging substance), then We made the alaqah into a mudghah (chewed lump), then We made the mudghah into bones, and then clothed the bones with flesh.' (23:13-14). Dr. Keith Moore confirmed these stages match modern embryological knowledge precisely.

Oceanography: 'He has set free the two seas meeting together. There is a barrier between them. They do not transgress.' (55:19-20). Modern science has discovered that where two seas meet, there is a physical barrier separating them due to differences in salinity, temperature, and density. This was unknown in the 7th century.

The water cycle: 'Do you not see that Allah drives clouds? Then He brings them together, then He makes them into a mass, and you see the rain emerge from within it.' (24:43). The detailed mechanics of the water cycle and cloud formation were not understood until the modern era.

Mountain roots: 'Have We not made the earth as a wide expanse, and the mountains as pegs?' (78:6-7). Modern geology has confirmed that mountains have deep roots beneath the surface, stabilizing the earth's crust — like pegs driven into the ground. This was unknown to 7th-century science.
