The Quran: The Miracle of Miracles

By Ahmed Deedat — an exploration of why the Quran, 1,400 years after its revelation, remains the greatest miracle given to the Prophet Muhammad and to all of humanity.

'Say: If the whole of mankind and jinn were to gather together to produce the like of this Quran, they could not produce the like thereof, even if they backed up each other with help and support.' (Quran 17:88). This is the Quran's bold challenge to all of humanity — a challenge that has remained unanswered for over 1,400 years.

Every prophet was given miracles appropriate to the age in which he lived. Moses lived in an age of magic, so his greatest miracle — his staff turning into a serpent — was in the realm of what the magicians of his time claimed mastery over. Jesus lived in an age when medicine and healing were the pinnacle of human achievement, so his miracles were in healing the sick and raising the dead.

Muhammad (peace be upon him) came in an age of eloquence and poetry. The Arabs were masters of language — they held poetry competitions at annual fairs, and the finest poems were inscribed in gold and hung in the Kaaba. The literary miracle of the Quran — its unparalleled eloquence, its unique style, its inimitable structure — was precisely calibrated to challenge the greatest experts of the time.

When the Arabs heard the Quran, they were stunned. They could not deny its literary beauty, yet they could not accept its message. So they said it was magic, or poetry, or the words of a madman — anything but what it was. The Quran responded: 'Say: Bring forth a chapter like it.' (2:23). Not a whole book — just one chapter. To this day, no one has met this challenge.

The Quran is not merely a literary miracle. It is a scientific miracle — containing knowledge that could not have been known in the 7th century. It is a historical miracle — accurately describing events of the distant past. It is a prophetic miracle — containing predictions that have been fulfilled. It is a mathematical miracle — containing numerical patterns of extraordinary complexity.

The continued existence of the Quran — unchanged, memorized, recited, and studied by billions across 14 centuries — is itself a miracle. The Quran says: 'Indeed, it is We who sent down the Reminder, and indeed, We will be its guardian.' (15:9). This divine promise has been kept.
