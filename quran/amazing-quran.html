The Amazing Quran

By Dr. Gary Miller — a compelling examination of the Quran's remarkable claims, its scientific accuracy, and what honest inquiry reveals about its divine origin.

Calling the Quran amazing is not something done only by Muslims, who have a special appreciation for it. Even people who are not sympathetic to the message of the Quran have nonetheless testified to its unique and extraordinary character.

Dr. Gary Miller, a Canadian mathematician and Christian theologian, set out to find errors in the Quran that he could use in his Christian missionary work. Instead, he found something that changed his life. He writes: 'One of the well-known theories of modern science is the Big Bang theory. Most people who have a general knowledge of science understand this theory. The Quran mentions this event in the following verse: Do not the unbelievers see that the heavens and the earth were joined together, then We clove them asunder? (21:30)'

The Quran's description of embryonic development — revealed 1,400 years before modern embryology — has stunned scientists. Dr. Keith Moore, former chairman of the Department of Anatomy at the University of Toronto, studied the Quranic verses on human embryology and stated: 'It is clear to me that these statements must have come to Muhammad from God, because almost all of this knowledge was not discovered until many centuries later.'
