The Quran and Modern Science

Dr. Maurice Bucaille's landmark study comparing the Quran with the findings of modern science — a work that changed millions of lives.

Dr. Maurice Bucaille was a French surgeon and scientist who spent many years studying the Quran in comparison with modern science. His book 'The Bible, The Quran and Science', published in 1976, became one of the most influential books about the Quran in the Western world and has been translated into dozens of languages.

Dr. Bucaille's central finding was that, while the Bible contains numerous scientific statements that are contradicted by modern knowledge, the Quran contains no statement that contradicts modern science. Moreover, it contains many statements that remarkably anticipate modern scientific discoveries.

He writes: 'The relationship between the Quran and science is a priori a surprise, especially when it turns out to be one of harmony and not of discord. A crucial problem confronts us: how could a text produced in the early seventh century A.D. deal with such a wide variety of subjects at a time when mythical beliefs still prevailed — and yet contain statements on the natural world that do not appear open to criticism in the light of modern scientific knowledge?'

Dr. Bucaille was particularly struck by the Quran's statements on human embryology. He writes: 'In contrast to the Bible, the Quran does not contain any statement that could be considered as being in contradiction with the firmly established facts of modern science.' After his extensive study, Dr. Bucaille became a Muslim.

The significance of his work is not that the Quran is a 'science book' — it is not. The Quran is primarily a book of guidance. But the fact that its occasional statements about the natural world are consistently accurate — and often far ahead of their time — is powerful evidence of its divine origin.

As Dr. Bucaille concluded: 'I could not find a single error in the Quran. I had to recognize the obvious: the Quran does not contain a single statement that is contrary to established modern knowledge — and there are many statements in the Quran that are in perfect harmony with modern science.' This consistency across all domains of knowledge points unmistakably to a source beyond human knowledge.
